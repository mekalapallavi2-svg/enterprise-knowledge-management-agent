Status: Active  

## Section 1: Personally Identifiable Information (PII)
Personally Identifiable Information (PII) refers to any data that can be used to distinguish or trace an individual's identity. Within ACME Corp, PII includes full names, employee IDs, social security numbers, home addresses, personal email addresses, and financial account details. PII must be encrypted at rest and in transit using AES-256 standard encryption. No PII should be stored on local drives or shared via unencrypted communications (such as Slack or public email).

## Section 2: GDPR and Data Subjects Rights
In compliance with GDPR, ACME Corp protects the rights of EU data subjects. These rights include:
1. The Right of Access: Data subjects can request copies of their personal data.
2. The Right to Erasure (Right to be Forgotten): Data subjects can request deletion of personal records when no longer required for legal obligations.
3. The Right to Rectification: Correction of incomplete or inaccurate data.
Any data subject request (DSR) must be forwarded to the Data Protection Officer (DPO) at dpo@acme.com within 24 hours of receipt. Resolutions must be finalized within 30 days.

## Section 3: Data Breach Response
In the event of a suspected or confirmed data breach involving PII, the incident must be reported immediately to the ACME Corp Security Operations Center (SOC). In compliance with GDPR Article 33, any breach causing risk to data subjects must be reported to the supervisory authority within 72 hours of detection. Affected individuals must be notified without undue delay.
